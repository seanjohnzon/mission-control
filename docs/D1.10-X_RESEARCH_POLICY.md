# D1.10 — X_RESEARCH_POLICY
**Autonomous AI Organization — How X/Twitter Research Enters the System, Is Filtered, Validated, and Routed**
_Last updated: 2026-03-11 | Status: Active | Parent: D1.1-ROADMAP.md_

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines how X/Twitter-derived research enters the organization, how it is filtered and validated, and under what conditions the Grok/xAI API may be activated as a research processing layer.

X is an important improvement and opportunity-discovery lane for this organization. It surfaces model and tool developments, architecture patterns, content opportunities, commerce signals, and early intelligence on changes that affect the system's strategic tracks. Used well, it accelerates the Experimentation and Intelligence Engine (Track 5). Used carelessly, it is noise that produces false urgency, premature tool changes, and drift.

**This document governs:**
- How X content is submitted to the organization (intake modes)
- Which sources are permitted and at what trust tier
- What a research packet is and what it must contain
- How signals are classified and validated
- What the Grok/xAI activation gate requires
- What valid outputs of X research look like
- How human-facing research summaries are formatted
- Automation boundaries for each intake mode

**What this document does not cover:**
- General content strategy or social media management — that is Track 2 (D1.8 §16)
- The full intake lane system — D5.1-INTAKE-LANES.md
- Experiment filing and outcome tracking — D5.4-EXPERIMENT-REGISTRY.md
- Grok API credentials — D1.14-SECRETS_POLICY.md
- Research delegation spec — D3.1-DELEGATION-SPEC-RESEARCH.md

## 2. Scope

This policy covers X content that enters the organization as research. X content used for other purposes (e.g. publishing to X as part of Track 2) is governed by D1.8 §16 Track 2, not by this document.

**In scope:**
- X posts, threads, and profile content submitted by the human
- X content processed by forge (Researcher) as part of a research task
- Grok/xAI API outputs used to analyze or extract signal from X content (when activated)

**Out of scope:**
- Posting to X or managing a presence on X — that is a Track 2 output operation
- LinkedIn, Reddit, newsletters, arXiv, or other research sources — those are part of the broader intake lane system in D5.1, not this document
- General web search or news research — separate from X research policy

## 3. Policy Principles

1. **X posts are not authoritative by default.** A post — regardless of the author's reputation — is an unvalidated claim until it is cross-referenced, tested, or confirmed by another source. No X post alone constitutes sufficient evidence to change architecture, policy, or production behavior.

2. **Signal extraction is the goal, not post collection.** The organization is not building a database of interesting tweets. It is extracting specific, actionable signals that can be routed to an appropriate destination: an experiment, a content idea, a capability evaluation, a market observation. Posts without extractable signal are noise.

3. **Noise is the default; signal is the exception.** The X environment is high-noise. When the system processes X content, it should assume most of it is not actionable. Aggressive filtering is a feature, not a shortcoming.

4. **Every finding passes through a research packet before it becomes anything else.** Raw post content never directly reaches the canonical memory store, the experiment registry, the content pipeline, or architecture documents. It goes through a research packet first. The packet is the filter.

5. **Raw posts never directly change production behavior.** No X post — not even one about a critical OpenClaw bug or a new model release — directly updates a policy document, modifies an agent configuration, or changes a running automation. It opens a research task, which may eventually result in a controlled change through the appropriate change control process (D1.15-CHANGE_CONTROL.md).

6. **Quality over volume.** A weekly digest of five validated, high-signal findings is more valuable than a daily flood of loosely filtered posts. The system must err on the side of extracting less and validating more.

7. **Grok is not active and is not assumed.** Phase 1 and Phase 2 X research operates without Grok. All processing uses local models or the human's own judgment. The Grok activation gate is defined in §9 and requires a specific set of conditions before it may be activated.

## 4. Intake Modes

X research enters the organization through three modes, each active at a different phase and each with a different automation level.

### Mode 1 — Manual Submission (Phase 1)
**Description:** The human pastes an X link, copies post text, attaches a screenshot, or types a summary of something they saw. This is submitted via Telegram to bridge (the primary coordination channel), which creates a research packet and routes it for processing.

**Who acts:** Human (source identification); bridge (packet creation); forge (Researcher) if processing is delegated, or human processes directly.

**Automation level:** None. The human initiates every intake event. Bridge assists with packet formatting.

**Phase:** Active from Phase 1.

### Mode 2 — Structured Intake (Phase 2)
**Description:** The human uses a defined intake template when submitting X content. The template includes fields for source URL or text, source account, perceived signal type, and priority. Bridge processes the template automatically: creates the research packet, routes to forge (Researcher) for extraction, and files the output.

**Who acts:** Human (source + template); bridge and forge (automated packet processing).

**Automation level:** Level 2 (supervised). Human submits; system processes and returns a summary for review. Requires a written delegation spec (D3.1) before activation.

**Phase:** Phase 2 target.

### Mode 3 — Automated Research Sweep (Phase 3)
**Description:** A defined list of high-signal X accounts or topics is monitored on a schedule. Forge (Researcher) processes new posts, extracts signal, and files findings without human initiation per item. The human receives a digest of validated findings.

**Who acts:** Forge (Researcher) — automated; bridge (Broker) — delivers digest; human — reviews digest and makes decisions.

**Automation level:** Level 2 required for sweep; individual validated findings that would trigger experiments require Level 3 approval for the resulting experiment, not for the sweep itself.

**Activation gate:** Mode 3 may not be activated until:
1. A curated account/topic list has been reviewed and approved by the human
2. Noise filtering thresholds have been confirmed through Mode 1 and Mode 2 experience
3. D5.1-INTAKE-LANES.md formalizes the research lane
4. A written delegation spec for automated research sweeps exists in D3.1
5. The circuit breaker in §13 is configured

**Phase:** Phase 3 target.

## 5. Allowed Sources

### 5.1 Source Types Permitted

| Source Type | Permitted | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Public X posts (any account) | Yes — with trust tier applied | Trust tier affects validation weight, not whether it can be submitted |
| X threads | Yes | Treated as a single research unit |
| X profile context (bio, pinned post) | Yes — for account credibility assessment only | Not a research source in itself |
| Grok-generated summaries of X content | Conditional | Only when Grok is activated per §9 |
| Screenshots of X content | Yes — with caveats | Must include account handle and post date; screenshots cannot be used alone as high-confidence evidence |

### 5.2 Source Types Prohibited

| Source Type | Prohibited | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Private/protected X accounts | Yes | Accessing protected content without consent is outside policy |
| DMs or private communications | Yes | Private communications are not research sources |
| Content requiring paid X subscription features to access | Yes | Credential exposure risk; declared in D1.14 if relevant |
| Synthetic or AI-generated posts presented as organic | Yes | Validation cannot be applied reliably; flag and discard |

### 5.3 Source Trust Tiers

Trust tier affects how much validation is required before a finding is treated as confirmed. It does not prevent submission.

| Tier | Description | Required Validation Before Acting |
|---|---|---|
| High | Account known to the human; track record of accurate, domain-relevant posts | One corroborating source or human judgment sufficient |
| Medium | Account unfamiliar but in a relevant domain; reasonable follower base; posts internally consistent | Two corroborating sources or forge cross-reference |
| Low | Unknown account, anonymous, or account with history of inaccurate posts | Strong corroboration required; cannot be sole basis for any action |
| Unverifiable | Screenshot with no visible account handle, or account since deleted | May not be used as evidence for any action; filed as `observation` only |

## 6. Research Packet Policy

Every piece of X content that enters the organization must be converted into a research packet before it is processed or acted on. A research packet is the structured form of a raw research input.

### 6.1 Required Fields

```
---
id: <unique identifier>
date_submitted: YYYY-MM-DD
submitted_by: human | forge
source_url: <X post URL or null>
source_account: <@handle or "unknown">
source_tier: high | medium | low | unverifiable
intake_mode: manual | structured | automated-sweep
signal_type: <from §7.1>
raw_content: <post text, screenshot reference, or summary>
signal_summary: <1–3 sentence extraction of the actionable finding>
validation_status: pending | confirmed | unconfirmed | contradicted | disputed
output_destination: <where this goes if validated — see §10>
---
```

### 6.2 Who Creates the Packet

| Intake Mode | Packet Creator |
|---|---|
| Mode 1 (manual) | Bridge creates the packet from the human's submission. Human reviews and corrects the `signal_summary` field before processing continues. |
| Mode 2 (structured) | Bridge creates the packet automatically from the intake template. No human review before processing, but human reviews the output. |
| Mode 3 (automated) | Forge (Researcher) creates the packet. Bridge logs it. Validated findings are included in the next digest for human review. |

### 6.3 Research Packets Are Not Memory Entries

Research packets are working documents, not canonical memory entries. A validated research packet that produces a finding is the input to a `knowledge` entry in canonical memory (via Archivist). The packet itself lives in working memory (forge) or operational memory (bridge) and is subject to those tiers' retention windows (D1.7 §10).

## 7. Signal Classification Policy

### 7.1 Signal Types

Every research packet is classified as one of the following signal types:

| Signal Type | Description | Potential Destination |
|---|---|---|
| `capability` | New model, tool, plugin, or capability relevant to the org's tech stack | Experiment proposal → D5.4; Capability evaluation → D1.12 |
| `architecture` | Pattern or approach used by others that may inform how the org builds | Architecture suggestion → D1.15 review queue |
| `content-opportunity` | Topic, format, or trend with high engagement relevant to Track 2 | Content track queue |
| `commerce-signal` | Product demand, pricing pattern, competitor activity relevant to Track 3 | brain (Merchant) queue |
| `risk` | Security vulnerability, legal development, market threat | Immediate escalation if critical; else queued for review |
| `openClaw` | OpenClaw-specific development, update, community discussion, bug report | Bridge update queue; architecture review if significant |
| `research-reference` | Academic paper, technical post, or deep-dive that expands a topic already in canonical memory | Canonical memory `knowledge` entry (via Archivist) |
| `noise` | Post with no extractable actionable signal after review | Discarded with reason logged |

### 7.2 Signal Strength

After classification, signal strength is assessed:

| Strength | Description | What Happens |
|---|---|---|
| High | Specific, falsifiable, directly relevant, from a credible source | Processed immediately; included in next digest |
| Moderate | Relevant but requires more evidence or context to act on | Filed for later; included in weekly digest |
| Low | Weakly relevant or speculative | Discarded unless part of an emerging pattern |
| Noise | Not actionable under any reasonable interpretation | Discarded; reason logged |

**Pattern escalation:** If 3 or more Low-signal posts from different sources converge on the same topic within a 7-day window, the cluster is re-evaluated as potential Moderate signal. The system does not discard clusters silently.

## 8. Validation Policy

### 8.1 Validation Requirement

No validated finding that would result in a system change (experiment, architecture suggestion, capability evaluation) may be based on a single unvalidated X post, regardless of the source's reputation. Validation is required before any finding moves from `pending` to `confirmed`.

### 8.2 Validation Criteria

A finding is considered validated when at least one of the following is true:
1. It can be cross-referenced against a second independent source (documentation, another post, a direct test)
2. It describes a testable claim that forge (Researcher) or the human can verify directly
3. The human explicitly marks it as confirmed based on their own knowledge

A finding is **not** validated solely because:
- It was posted by a high-follower account
- It was widely shared
- It aligns with what the organization hoped to find
- It cannot be immediately disproven

### 8.3 Validation Outputs

| Status | Meaning | What Happens |
|---|---|---|
| `confirmed` | Evidence supports the finding | Route to declared output destination (§10) |
| `unconfirmed` | Insufficient evidence; not disproven | Hold; add to research backlog for further investigation |
| `contradicted` | Evidence contradicts the finding | Discard; log the contradiction in case the topic recurs |
| `disputed` | Mixed evidence; competing claims | File as `observation` in canonical memory noting the dispute; do not act on it |

### 8.4 Who Validates

| Finding Type | Who Validates |
|---|---|
| High Signal findings that would affect architecture or policy | Human validates before output is routed |
| High Signal findings that feed experiments | Forge (Researcher) validates via cross-reference; human reviews the experiment proposal before it is filed to D5.4 |
| Moderate Signal findings | Forge (Researcher) validates; result included in digest for human awareness |
| Low Signal / Noise | Forge (Researcher) classifies and discards; no human review required |

The human is never the sole validator for Low/Noise signals — that would recreate the bottleneck this system is designed to remove.

## 9. Research Processing Capability Gates

### 9.0 Perplexity — Alternative Research Processing Capability

Perplexity (D5.7 CAP-006) is a candidate external callable capability that may serve as a research processing layer within this pipeline, complementary to Grok with different strengths: live web retrieval, current news, and structured search.

**Current status:** Not activated. Candidate only. See D5.7 CAP-006 and TASK-013, TASK-014.

**If activated, Perplexity's role in this pipeline would be:**
- Live web retrieval for research packet validation (cross-referencing claims against current sources)
- Mode 2 and Mode 3 research processing where live retrieval adds signal that local models cannot provide
- Augmenting research briefs with current information not in static model weights

**Constraints that apply regardless of research processing tool:**
- The §8 validation policy applies to all external research sources and all processing tools — Perplexity outputs are not authoritative by default
- Findings processed by Perplexity follow the same packet format (§6), signal classification (§7), and validation criteria (§8) as any other finding
- Perplexity does not replace forge (Researcher) — it is a callable helper invoked by forge or bridge within research tasks, not an independent agent

**Activation gate:** Perplexity activation follows D1.12 capability acquisition independently of the Grok gate in §9.3.

---

### 9.1 Current Status

**Grok is not activated.** The Research-External model class (D1.5 §4) is defined but inactive. No xAI API calls are made at Phase 1 or Phase 2.

**Note on Perplexity:** Perplexity (D5.7 CAP-006) is a separate candidate capability for live web research. If activated, it may serve as a research processing layer within this pipeline (cross-referencing claims, live source retrieval, structured web search). It is governed independently of the Grok activation gate — see §9.0 above. All §8 validation rules apply to Perplexity outputs as they do to any research source.

### 9.2 What Grok Would Do If Activated

If activated, Grok/xAI API is used exclusively as a research processing layer within this pipeline:
- Structured extraction of signal from raw X post content
- Topic classification and trend identification across a set of posts
- Cross-referencing claims against xAI's training data (with the same validation requirements as any other source — Grok outputs are not authoritative by default)

Grok is not used for: policy decisions, agent configuration, financial analysis, production task execution, or any purpose outside the X research intake pipeline.

### 9.3 Activation Requirements

All of the following must be true before Grok may be activated:

| # | Requirement | Status |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | D1.10-X_RESEARCH_POLICY.md is written and approved | Satisfied by this document |
| 2 | D1.14-SECRETS_POLICY.md is written and the xAI credential class is governed by it — the actual xAI key value is obtained, registered, and stored only after all other gate conditions are satisfied and human activation approval (condition 3) is given | Not yet satisfied — D1.14 is written; key not yet obtained |
| 3 | Human explicitly approves Grok activation in writing (Telegram, Discord, or Mission Control record) | Not yet satisfied |
| 4 | A research experiment spec for Grok's use is filed in D5.4-EXPERIMENT-REGISTRY.md | Not yet satisfied |
| 5 | Grok tasks are declared as Research-External class in a task packet; routing follows D1.5 §5 | Not yet satisfied |

If any requirement is unmet, Grok is not activated. There is no partial activation.

**Sequencing note on condition 2:** The xAI key value must not be obtained or stored before conditions 3–5 are also met and the human has given activation approval. The governance framework (D1.14 written, xAI class defined) satisfies condition 2 as a policy prerequisite. The actual credential lifecycle — obtain → register metadata → store in approved location → set active — is executed immediately after approval, not in advance of it. This prevents a circular dependency where the key must exist before the gate approves its existence. (Source: D1.14 §14, §10.1)

### 9.4 Grok Cost and Usage

If activated, Grok API calls are governed by D1.5 §6 (cost policy) and are counted against the Research-External model class budget. Token usage is logged per call. If costs exceed a declared threshold, Grok calls are suspended and the human is notified.

## 10. Output Policy

A validated research finding from this pipeline produces one or more of the following outputs. Each output type maps to a defined destination in the organization.

| Output Type | Description | Destination | Who Files |
|---|---|---|---|
| Research brief | A structured summary of a validated finding or a cluster of related findings | Canonical memory `knowledge` entry via Archivist (D1.7 §5) | Forge (Researcher) → Bridge → Archivist |
| Experiment proposal | A proposal to test a capability, architecture idea, or tool discovered via X research | D5.4-EXPERIMENT-REGISTRY.md (pending human approval before filing) | Forge (Researcher) drafts; human approves before filing |
| Content opportunity note | A validated topic, format, or trend that Track 2 should evaluate | Content track queue in Mission Control | Forge → Bridge → content queue |
| Architecture suggestion | A pattern or approach that may inform how the org builds | D1.15-CHANGE_CONTROL.md review queue | Forge drafts; human reviews before it enters the queue |
| Commerce/market note | A market signal relevant to Track 3 | brain (Merchant) queue | Forge → Bridge → brain task queue |
| Discarded | No actionable signal | Logged with reason; not filed anywhere else | Forge discards; reason retained in working memory until task closes |

**One finding, one destination.** A single research packet produces the highest-priority applicable output type. It does not flood multiple queues simultaneously. If a finding is relevant to multiple tracks, the research brief is the destination — track-specific teams read the brief.

## 11. Human-Facing UX Policy

### 11.1 What the Human Receives

The human should receive exactly enough information to make a decision or take note of a finding. They should not receive a wall of raw post content, a list of everything that was processed, or a detailed summary of everything that was discarded.

### 11.2 Per-Finding Message Format

For High Signal findings, bridge posts a summary to Telegram (primary coordination channel) in this format:

```
Research signal: [signal type]
Source: [account handle or "anonymous"] | Trust: [tier]
Finding: [1–2 sentences — the actionable insight only]
Validation: [confirmed / unconfirmed / disputed]
Filed to: [destination]
Action needed: [yes — describe / no]
```

Nothing else. No post text. No screenshot. No model output. The human reads the finding. If they want the source, they ask.

### 11.3 Digest Format for Batched Findings

Moderate and Low Signal findings are not sent individually. They are batched into a weekly research digest. The digest format:

```
Research digest — week of YYYY-MM-DD
X research lane: [N] packets processed | [N] confirmed | [N] discarded

High signal (included above as individual notices): [N]

Moderate signal:
- [Finding 1: 1 sentence] — filed to [destination]
- [Finding 2: 1 sentence] — filed to [destination]

Noise this week: [N] posts discarded — [dominant noise category if notable]
```

### 11.4 What Bridge Never Sends to the Human from This Pipeline

- Raw X post text or quote-tweets
- Lists of posts that were checked and discarded
- Model reasoning or classification outputs
- Research packets in their raw form
- Repeated notices for the same finding in different digest cycles

## 12. Automation Boundaries

Automation boundaries for X research are governed by D1.8 and are summarized here for this specific pipeline.

| Mode | Automation Class | Risk Level | Approved | Gate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mode 1 (manual submission) | Class 2 (triggered) | Level 1–2 | Level 0 — human triggers per submission; system assists | No delegation spec required; bridge processes by default |
| Mode 2 (structured intake) | Class 3 (delegated single-step) | Level 2 | Level 2 (supervised) — requires delegation spec | D3.1 must exist; human reviews each output before next run |
| Mode 3 (automated sweep) | Class 1 (scheduled) + Class 4 (multi-step) | Level 2 for sweep; Level 3 for any resulting external action | Level 2 minimum; Level 3 for content or capability actions | Curated source list approved; D3.1 exists; D5.1 formalized; circuit breaker configured |
| Grok-assisted processing | Class 3 (delegated) | Level 2 (analysis only) | Conditional — activation gate §9 not satisfied | All §9 requirements must be met |

**What is never automated at any maturity level:**
- Validation of High Signal findings that would affect architecture or policy — human reviews these
- Filing an architecture suggestion to the D1.15 review queue without human sign-off
- Activating a new capability or tool based solely on an X research finding

## 13. Risk / Noise Controls

### 13.1 Noise Threshold

If any automated sweep (Mode 3) produces a noise rate greater than 70% in a single run, the sweep is paused and bridge posts an alert: "Research sweep pause: noise threshold exceeded ([X]% noise in last run). Review source list before resuming." The sweep does not restart automatically.

### 13.2 Source List Integrity

The curated source list for Mode 3 is owned by the human. Forge does not add accounts to the monitored list. Bridge does not add accounts to the monitored list. The list is a human-maintained document. If a new account is discovered through research and appears valuable, it is flagged as a recommendation in a digest — not added automatically.

### 13.3 Maximum Findings Per Run

Every research task packet (Modes 2 and 3) must declare a maximum number of findings it will surface. Default maximum: 10 findings per run. If more than the declared maximum would qualify, the highest-signal items are selected and the rest are discarded with a count noted in the digest. This prevents a single sweep from overwhelming the human's attention.

### 13.4 Recency Check

Before processing any post, the research packet must record the post's publication date. Posts older than 90 days are tagged `aged` and require explicit justification to be treated as current signal. Rediscovered old posts are common in algorithmic feeds and should not be treated as new developments.

### 13.5 Claim Specificity Requirement

A finding must be specific before it is treated as Moderate or High Signal. Vague directional claims ("AI is changing everything," "this model is amazing") are classified as noise regardless of source. A claim that names a specific model, tool, behavior, or capability with testable implications is eligible for classification as signal.

### 13.6 No Bandwagon Validation

A finding does not gain validity from the number of people sharing it. Viral posts are subject to the same validation requirements as obscure posts. Popularity on X is not evidence.

## 15. Immediate Priorities

### Phase 1 — before any research automation

1. **Practice Mode 1 manual intake for 2–4 weeks** — The human submits X findings manually via Telegram. Bridge creates research packets. Forge (or the human directly) extracts signal. No automation. No Grok. The goal is to learn what the noise-to-signal ratio actually looks like and what the human finds valuable. This is the foundation for calibrating everything that comes later. (Source: §4 Mode 1)

2. **Define and test the research packet template** — Create the intake template the human will use in Mode 1. Test it with 3–5 real submissions. Refine the `signal_summary` field until it produces consistently useful 1–3 sentence extractions. (Source: §6)

3. **Identify 5–10 high-signal X accounts manually** — Based on Mode 1 experience, the human compiles a first draft of the curated source list. This is not automation — it is the human knowing which accounts are consistently useful. This list becomes the seed for Mode 3 when the time comes. (Source: §13.2)

4. **Write D3.1-DELEGATION-SPEC-RESEARCH.md** — The delegation spec for research tasks is the prerequisite for Mode 2 and Mode 3. It cannot be written correctly without Mode 1 experience informing it. Write it after 2–4 weeks of manual intake. (Source: §4 Mode 2 gate)

5. **Do not activate Grok** — The §9 activation gate is not satisfied. Record this as a known future option, not a current priority. (Source: §9.1)

### Phase 2 — after Mode 1 experience is established

6. **Write D5.1-INTAKE-LANES.md** — Formalize the X research lane alongside other intake lanes (YouTube, arXiv, newsletters). X research does not stand alone — it is one lane in a broader intake system. (Source: §4 Mode 3 gate)

7. **Activate Mode 2 (structured intake)** — Once D3.1 exists and Mode 1 experience has informed the packet template, activate Mode 2. First runs are supervised (Level 2). (Source: §4 Mode 2, §12)

8. **Calibrate noise and signal thresholds** — Set the Mode 3 circuit breaker threshold, the maximum findings per run, and the digest cadence based on Mode 2 experience. These are not guesswork — they require real data. (Source: §13.1, §13.3)
